Areas of Application:

Description:
OrCam is an intuitive reading tool with a hint. The device consists of a small camera mounted on the glasses, which recognizes texts from up to 30 m away and discreetly reads over the auditory bone. With the battery charge, the user is independent throughout the day and hands are free for interaction. Alternatively, the buttons of the belt-mounted control unit can be used instead of a finger to read information read.

While blind people may not use the device like people with the most serious, regular exercise and tips will help. For example, documents can be held directly against the nose and moved backwards to ensure that the text is captured.

The device is a combination of image sensor module, control computer and bone sound speaker and looks like a pair of glasses. The system recognizes the focused object, processes it in text information and passes this text on to the wearer via the bone sound speaker.

Orcam is available in two models:
The standard version Orcam MyReader focuses on pure text recognition. OrCam MyEye currently offers face and product recognition as additional features.
The microphone allows the user to name persons and products. On the control panel, which offers storage space at the same time, up to 100 persons and 150 products can be stored.

At work, for example, the device can be useful for attending meetings, accessing the file or going to work.

- can read texts in newspapers, on packaging as well as signs and posters
